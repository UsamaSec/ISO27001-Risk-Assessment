# ISO 27001 Risk Assessment — Mock Healthcare Clinic

A mock information security risk assessment for a fictional small healthcare clinic, conducted using ISO/IEC 27001 methodology — asset identification, risk scoring, and treatment recommendations mapped to Annex A controls, plus a Statement of Applicability (SoA).

## Why This Project

This project applies the GRC and ISO 27001 methodology I used in my Data Security Specialist Assistant role at AURISCON GmbH to a full, self-directed risk assessment exercise — demonstrating the end-to-end process: identifying assets, assessing risk, selecting controls, and documenting justification, the same structured approach used in real ISO 27001 compliance work.

## Scenario
A fictional small healthcare clinic ("Riverside Family Clinic") handling electronic patient health records, appointment scheduling, and billing. Chosen specifically because healthcare data carries high sensitivity and strict regulatory expectations, making for a realistic, high-stakes risk assessment exercise.

## Methodology
Based on ISO/IEC 27001:2022 risk assessment principles:
1. Define scope and context
2. Identify and classify information assets
3. Identify threats and vulnerabilities per asset
4. Assess risk (likelihood × impact)
5. Determine risk treatment (mitigate, accept, transfer, avoid)
6. Select and justify Annex A controls (Statement of Applicability)
